Oysters belong to the families Ostreidae and Aviculidae. They are used to the salty seawater well. Furthermore, they can grow up in the estuary well. It is suitable to farm oyster along the coast from Rouzongjiao to Shen-Gang. Farming oyster relies on the tide: when the tide is on the flow, oysters are in the seawater; when tide is on the ebb, the farmers work under the racks. The management is very convenient.

Oyster farming in Rouzongjaio most adapt the style of hanging on the racks. This style is to set the oyster larvae near the area of the flow and ebb of the tide, in lower area or in the ocean trench. They make the spats grow out until they grow up to able to “plant” in deep water. It almost takes about ten months to produce oyster larvae, protect the spat and farm them in natural water. The time of producing oyster larvae is at the end of November each year. The oyster farmers will set the spat on the rack, 12 shells in plastic mesh bags, in costal estuary for about four months, and then move them to the deeper area. When next June comes, the farmers will harvest.


The oyster farmer, Cing-Tian Ke, said, “because the weather is changeable these years, the oyster larvae grow up slowly. However it is a challenge for the oyster larvae. If the larvae live in this situation, it will grow up in the future.”

However, because the price of the oysters is 135 NT each kilogram, the oyster farmers think it is a good price as to adding the amount of farming. Therefore, the plastic mesh bags and racks are not enough to provide the farmers’ need.

As for tying the plastic bags up, it is always done by the old generation. This is because the young generation doesn’t have the skills and the salary is less. According to another oyster farmer’s words, the workers usually reuse the oyster shells, which are left when the oyster meat is taken out. After exposing to the sunlight for a period of time, they will choose the big and whole ones to reduce. In addition, the dealers afford the racks, ropes, and plastic bags. A worker, owning good experiences, can tie three or four hundred strings, one NT each string tying. However, this is a good income for the ole people, and they also can kill time.

If you come here when the tide is on the flow, you will see many oyster racks stick out the sea. On the other hand, when the tide is on the ebb, you can see the widen oyster farm, farming cars and diligent farmers. The oyster shells are piled into a small mountain beside the road. Thereupon, next time, if you visit here, you shall take the oyster farming car and have a look on the view of the oyster farm. You also can purchase some fresh oysters back home to cook and taste.
