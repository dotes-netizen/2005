Introduction to the head of the township: Huang, Hong-Yao
Mr. Huang is a local resident who was born and grew up in Hsien-Hsi. Because his father died while he was a child and was raised up by his mother, he determined to contribute himself to serve the neighborhood. After the army service, he had a part-time job and also studied at the same time. In 1979, he passed an exam and had the chance to become a government employee. After that, He had served in several towns, including, Er-Lin, Homei, Hsien-Hei, and Shen-Gang. Therefore, he has a deeply understanding and experiences of human geography. Being the head of the Hsien-Hsi township after 2003, he is active in constructing, and now the efforts can be seen in three aspects: community construction, community welfare, and using the Internet to help people.

 

1. What’s your opinion about the coast along Hsien-Hsi?
Mr. Huang has lived here for more than fifty years, and compared with before, he isn’t satisfied with the coast now. He says Rouzongjiao used to be very famous. At sunset, couples went there to recall memory, and friends get together to have fun. People could enjoy the breathtaking scenery. However, the coast belongs to the national property now, so the township administration can’t do anything about it nowadays. The development of Chang-Hau Industrial Area and the oyster racks around the coast damage the scenes. The coast now isn’t wonderful at all.
2. What is the township administration’s authority for local construction, and does Wunzai Port belong to the administration or the government?
Roughly speaking, the township administration is responsible for community building up and facilities, such as the library, and health center. As for the coast, it’s the government’s property. For example, the authority of developing Wunzai Port goes to upper administration. The township administration can only support.
The head of the township says the government doesn’t think Wunzai Port as a port, but an inlet. In fact, Wunzai Port is the under-developed area, and the west part of its surface is the extension of Ching-An drain. In early times, Wunzai was a place where fishermen took a rest. And women sold their fish by carrying bamboo bag and peddling around, so some residents even didn’t know the place. Until 2000, the township starts to promote and advertise, and in 2001, visitors began to appear. After the mullet festival in 2002 held by the township administration, Wunzai Port becomes very famous and the government even gave 200000 dollars as activity pension at that time.
3. What’s your opinion about the development and plans along the Hsien-His coast?

The clam camp is what the administration fully works for actively. There is two hectares of national plain in Hsien-His village. In the 1940’s, Veterans Affairs Commission settled some old soldiers who are in their fifty’s to cultivate clams. Because they were governed by the army and also wore the uniforms, so people called them “clam soldiers” and the place where they lived is “clam camp.” In 1980’s, the old soldiers left and this area was abandoned. Now, only about ten soldiers with their families live here.

According to the ideal “Turning dirty places into clean places, cleaning points getting together to become a line, and then forming a scenery area, making the corridor form Shen-Gang, Hsien-His, to Homei an attractive resort, the head of township would like to combine the clam camp with visiting resorts. Now the plans are trying to make mills become the landmark of Hsien-Hsi, and make Hsien-Hsi the middle resting station on the west coastal freeway. In order to increase business, we will provide B&B to let visitors feel the way of life the farmer and fishermen lived here. The eleven rooms is going to be established in clam camp an each room introduces different resort and culture in Hsien-His such as drum beating, horse ranches, and preserved eggs. We also want to connect resorts in Ding-Jhuang, horse ranches, Hsien-His park, Yong-An drum-making factory, and Wunzai old church together to form a series of visiting. However, because we haven’t got the usage of the land, there is no pension to support the construction.
4. What is the future plan for Hsien-Hsi Coast?
About Wunazi Port:
The head of township thinks the most important plan for the future Wunzai Pork is to set up rules betweens fishermen. This enables them to treat customers with honesty. Besides, the fishermen’s organization isn’t well-organized, so if it can be run systematicly, have connection with relevant units and hold get-together activities between fishermen, Wunzai Port will be more popular.

About Rouzongjiao:
As for this part, Rouzongjiao is only for industrial use, so the township administration doesn’t have the right to see it. However, there is no pension for the place to develop, so the administration had offered a three-duration project to the industry area, that is, to borrow land to beautify Ching-An drain. The head of township mentions that the hardest part is to get the land.
5. What difficulties do we face in the processing of developing?
The head of township thinks in the process, we have two main problems: the first one is the development of industry area and visiting is a dilemma. So far, the government still can’t make environment protection, industry area, and visiting resorts exist at the same time. The second problem is the residents’ dependence on each others. For example, the fishermen at Wunzai Port aren’t solidary and don’t look for new development actively at all. On the contrary, they expect the township administration to hold activities for them
Besides, the moving-out problem more or less affects the construction. Once the population decreases; so do the consumption and tax. The main reasons to cause this problem are the development of Changhau Coastal Industrial Park isn’t good and so is the crop. The young generation wants to do jobs at higher level, so they choose to move out. Recently, the population increases slightly, about 17000.
6. What are the difficulties of the development of Hsien-His coast?
The head of township thinks the most serious problem is the unconvenience of traffic. After Changhau Coastal Industrial Park is developed, each car, which would like to enter, should be all under control. Besides, the resorts are all behind the Industrial Park, so seldom visitors want to go there. Another developing difficulty is the landform limitation. The alongshore coast is sharp; only Rouzongjiao is flat, but the residents near Rouzongjiao set up oyster racks to cultivate. This damages the scenery. Therefore, the plans for the coast so far is to hope people to get oyster racks back and ask visitors to have civism to keep the beach clean and save the only resort. However, the government does nothing because they think Hsien-Hsi beach is not worth investing. It is really a pity.
7. How does the county government help to complete plan? What is the actual action? How about the affection?
Generally speaking, government’s developing plan is whole-designed, for example the widening plan for the main road form Hsien-His to Homei. Hsien-Hsi is not only at “Fongtousshueiwei,” and hideaway, but also without resort and speciality. Therefore the pension is always less. However, in 2003, the township administration introduced golden sweet potatoes to farmers and they had a great harvest, so we got government pension this time.
8. What is the next step for the whole community construction?
The head of township says, when we get the pension, the clam camp is the first thing to do, and then the rebuilding of the health center. The health center was built in Japanese time. He proposes to rebuild it gradually and make it become a place for refresher course to improve the prosperity. We have got the pension for the clam camp, 900000 dollars these days, and the money is enough for us to reconstruct and advertise. We will create a new future for Hsien-His beginning from this resort.
